Pillar Three

Enterprise

Creating opportunity. Not just finding it — making it.

Beyond Employment, Into Enterprise

Most veteran networks stop at employment. They help people find roles, make introductions, and move on. The ROSE Network goes further — because its members do.

Enterprise is the third pillar of the network: the space where professional connection becomes commercial collaboration. Where members do not just access opportunity — they create it. Starting businesses. Forming consulting partnerships. Taking advisory roles. Working together commercially with people they already trust.

This is what happens when you build a network of people with operational discipline, analytical capability, and professional ambition — and then give them the structure to work together.

What Enterprise Looks Like

Starting Businesses

Members launching ventures — independently or together — with the support, introductions, and credibility that a trusted professional network provides.

Consulting Partnerships

Forming consulting and advisory partnerships with fellow members whose complementary expertise and shared professional standards make collaboration natural.

Advisory Roles

Experienced members taking advisory and non-executive positions — contributing strategic insight shaped by operational experience to boards and leadership teams.

Why BVO Exists

The ROSE Network operates within the Intelligence Corps Association, a registered charity. Commercial activity — businesses, partnerships, paid advisory work — cannot and should not sit inside a charitable structure.

BVO is the dedicated commercial vehicle that makes enterprise possible without compromising the network's charitable purpose. It provides the structure for members to work together commercially, while the ROSE Network continues to operate as a trust-based professional community under the ICA.

This separation is deliberate. The charity facilitates connection and support. BVO enables creation and commerce. Together, they give the network a reach that neither could achieve alone.

The Progression

Enterprise does not exist in isolation. It is the natural extension of a network that already works.

1

Transition

You enter the network. You receive mentoring, sector insight, and the confidence to move into civilian professional life.

2

Professional Network

You contribute. You connect. You build a career surrounded by people who share your background and understand your value.

3

Enterprise

You create. You build businesses, form partnerships, and work commercially with people you already trust — through BVO.

Ready to Build Something?

If you are an Intelligence Corps veteran with ambition beyond employment — if you want to start a business, form a partnership, or take an advisory role — the Enterprise pillar and BVO provide the structure to make it happen.
