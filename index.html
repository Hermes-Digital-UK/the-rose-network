About the ROSE Network

The ROSE Network exists to support the Intelligence Corps community through professional connection, mentoring, and career transition guidance. It operates as a function of the Intelligence Corps Association (ICA), Registered Charity No. 1175211.

Formerly known as ICAREC, the network was rebranded to reflect that its purpose extends beyond resettlement for serving personnel. The ROSE Network serves the whole Corps family: Regulars, Reserves, Veterans, and immediate family members.

Core Functions

Support Transition

Guiding serving personnel as they prepare for civilian careers through mentoring and practical advice.

Enable Connection

Facilitating introductions between veterans, serving personnel, and professionals across industry and government.

Sustain the Community

Maintaining a lifelong professional network that endures beyond service and supports members at every stage.

The Two-Pillar Model

ROSE Transition

For Serving Personnel

Supporting those in their final 24 months of service. ROSE Transition provides access to mentoring from experienced veterans, guidance on career preparation, and structured introductions to professionals working in relevant sectors.

The ROSE Network does not provide job brokerage, placement services, or any form of commercial recruitment activity.

ROSE Veteran Network

For Intelligence Corps Veterans

A professional alumni network for those who have completed their service. The Veteran Network sustains connection through peer support, mentoring, professional introductions, and engagement at ROSE Forums and events.

The ROSE Network facilitates introductions only. It does not broker contracts, act as an intermediary, or provide commercial referral services.

Mentoring & Transition

Mentoring is the primary mechanism through which the ROSE Network delivers value. It connects those preparing for civilian life with experienced veterans who have navigated similar transitions.

Veteran-Led

Mentors are Intelligence Corps veterans with direct experience of transition and civilian careers.

Judgement-Based

Guidance is tailored, candid, and grounded in real professional experience rather than generic frameworks.

Time-Bound

Mentoring engagements are structured with a clear purpose and defined duration, not open-ended commitments.

Purpose-Driven

Every engagement is directed towards a practical outcome, whether career preparation, sector insight, or professional development.

Transition support encompasses guidance on CV preparation, interview skills, relevant courses and qualifications, and understanding the professional landscape beyond the military. The network draws on the collective experience of its veteran members to offer honest, practical counsel.

Events & Engagement

ROSE Forums

The ROSE Network hosts structured forums across the United Kingdom, providing an opportunity for serving personnel and veterans to meet, exchange professional insight, and build lasting connections.

Forums are invitation-led, low-cost, and high-value. They combine candid discussion with informal follow-on, creating an environment where genuine professional relationships can develop naturally.

ROSE Buds

Over time, the network will develop regional engagement through volunteer representatives — known as ROSE Buds — who provide a local point of contact and help sustain the network's reach beyond the annual forums.

Opportunities

From time to time, members and industry contacts share employment opportunities and professional openings with the ROSE Network.

These are passed on in good faith as a service to the community. The network serves as a channel for information, not as an active intermediary in any recruitment or selection process.

The ROSE Network does not act as a recruitment broker, commercial intermediary, or employment agency. All opportunities are shared for information only.

Governance

The ROSE Network operates within the governance framework of the Intelligence Corps Association. It reports through established ICA structures to the Association's trustees and is subject to the same charitable obligations and standards of conduct.

The network is guided by a set of operating principles: light-touch governance, volunteer-led delivery, trust-based relationships, and a commitment to avoid duplicating existing provision. These principles ensure that the network remains focused, sustainable, and aligned with the interests of the Corps community it serves.

The Intelligence Corps Association is a registered charity (No. 1175211) with the Charity Commission for England and Wales.
