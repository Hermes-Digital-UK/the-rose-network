# The Rose Network — Project Context

## Organisation
**Full name:** ICA Resettlement & Support for Employment 'Rose' Network (formerly ICAREC)
**Short name:** The Rose Network
**Parent:** Intelligence Corps Association (ICA)
**Chair:** Stephen James (full autonomy over Rose Network digital)

## Background
ICAREC rebranded to The Rose Network to emphasise that employment support extends beyond resettlement for Serving personnel. It serves the whole Corps Family: Regulars, Reserves, Veterans, and immediate family.

## Core Services (three pillars)

### 1. Employment Opportunities
Up-to-date employment opportunities passed to The Rose Network by the extensive Civilian Network.

### 2. Careers Information
Guidance for Service Leavers on transition from the military, including:
- CV production
- Interview techniques
- Relevant courses and training requirements

### 3. Virtual Employment Network (VEN)
A new initiative supplementing the five annual Forums. Service Leavers explore employment types (Accountant to Security Consultant) via a drop-down menu, fill in an electronic form with experience, leaving date, and other details. This anonymously sends their information to a Networker currently employed in that field, who then contacts the Leaver directly with transition advice.

## Annual Forums
Five events per year held across the country:
- London (x2)
- Bristol
- Manchester
- Edinburgh

Forums provide Leavers the opportunity to meet Network community members in a relaxed environment. Networkers offer advice, mentoring, and occasional employment opportunities. Last hosted by Deloitte in London.

## Constraints
- No client/member data in personal or shared environments
- Member data (especially Service Leavers, Networkers) is highly sensitive
- VEN submissions must be handled anonymously until Networker contact

## Design Notes
- Tone: professional, supportive, military-aware, accessible
- British English throughout
- Must feel trustworthy — audience includes serving personnel and veterans
